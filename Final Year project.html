FINAL YEAR PROJECT

Project Name:

Characterization of Brake Pad Composite Materials by Varying the Composition of Epoxy and Natural Fibres


Abstract:

The application of composite materials to brake pads is becoming increasingly popular with their remarkable performance properties, including thermal stability, wear resistance, and noise reduction. Nevertheless, research is still working on improving new composite materials to achieve even better performance. In this research, composites were developed through the hand layup process with epoxy resin used as the matrix with Sugarcane Bagasse, Pineapple Fiber, and Glass Fiber as reinforcement. The amount of the composites was manipulated by changing the reinforcing material percentages. Different tests were performed such as Tensile, Flexural Strength, and Impact Tests. The test results showed that reinforcement materials incorporation in the epoxy resin matrix increased the mechanical characteristics of the composites. The general observation of this study is that the hand layup process is a good approach to fabricating brake pad composite materials, and adding rice husk, hemp, sugarcane, and pineapple fibers can enhance further these characteristics. The sample specimen containing a blend of 50 weight percent, 25 weight percent, 15 weight percent, and 15 weight percent yielded the best results. Since it encourages innovation, ensures sustainability, and minimizes emissions in the automotive industry, this research aligns with the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).

Index Terms:

Sugarcane bagasse, Pineapple fiber, Hemp, Rice huck, Epoxy, Tensile strength, Flexural strength, Impact test, Sustainable materials and Wear resistance.
